    The Oligarchs Rules

    The Oligarchs is a game of investment, war, and political intrigue. At its core, it is merely a fairly simple turn-based strategy game: nations build factories, collect income, recruit troops, and go to war. The novelty is that each player doesn't control their own country - they buy shares in multiple countries and have to cooperate democratically to get anything done!

    The game proceeds along these stages:

    1. Taxation - the bank pays money out to each nation according to how many tiles and factories it controls
    2. Discussion - players discuss alliances and future plans
    3. Auction - players bid on a share for each country in order (NA, SA, EU, AF, AS, AU)
    4. Action
      1. Election - shareholders elect the president
      2. Movement - the president moves existing troops
      3. Attack - the president attacks with existing troops
      4. Spawn - the president recruits new troops at barracks
      5. Build - the president builds new factories and barracks
      6. Dividends - the president pays a country's cash out to its shareholders in proportion to the number of shares owned

    Finally, another novel twist that The Oligarchs is that the order countries act in flips every round. Thus, while the order on odd rounds (#1, #3, #5) is [NA, SA, EU, AF, AS, AU], the order on even rounds (#2, #4, #6) is [AU, AS, AF, EU, SA, NA]. This has an important effects on gameplay: each country will have two turns to act before another country acts, which makes military attacks much harder to respond to in a timely fashion.
